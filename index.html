04 · Oxford projects

Course-based research in depth.

Selected projects completed through the University of Oxford Department of Biochemistry.

Functional genomics

Genome perturbation

Interpreted C. elegans AIR-2 variants through genomic annotation, cross-species sequence alignment, and kinase-domain conservation. Compared feeding RNAi with a balanced air-1 loss-of-function allele using progeny phenotyping, linkage analysis, and whole-mount DAPI imaging to relate Aurora-kinase disruption to embryonic lethality and germline defects.

Performed Cas9–homology-directed editing of E. coli lacZ and designed candidate guide RNAs for human PCSK9, evaluating PAM compatibility, sequence-dependent off-target risk, and strategies for confirming precise edits.

Structural & quantitative

Computational biochemistry

Processed a selenomethionine CD44 diffraction dataset and performed molecular replacement and refinement, then used Coot and Phenix to dock and rebuild an X-ray-derived PFN2 domain within a cryo-EM density map.

Ran atomistic and coarse-grained GROMACS simulations of glycophorin A transmembrane-helix association, comparing time-dependent contacts and membrane-dependent dynamics with an experimental NMR structure.

Applied Fiji- and R-based workflows to quantify EdU-labelled nuclei, protein–DNA gel shifts, and macromolecular-binding titrations, assessing how segmentation, ligand depletion, concentration range, and model complexity affected parameter uncertainty.

Protein biochemistry

Construct design & characterisation

Integrated homology searches, disorder and topology predictions, and structural models to define candidate constructs and expression strategies for bovine histone H1 and Trypanosoma brucei ISG65.

Purified a His-tagged receptor by Ni-NTA chromatography and used Coomassie-stained SDS-PAGE and Western blotting to distinguish product purity from target-protein recovery; subsequently evaluated candidate ligand interactions using an AviTag-mediated bead pull-down assay.
